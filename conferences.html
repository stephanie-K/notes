User Vision - Deisgn for the future - 2019

World Usability Day 2019

Discussion of the panels – Insights

  • Roan Lavery (Free agent) You have to be aware of how your customers see you: Skyscanner see themselves as a travel agency, but the customers see them as ‘only’ flights sellers – something they are trying to change (Oli Mival – UR head at Skyskanner)
  • Blockbusters were seeing themselves as a DVD and video providers, and they failed to adapt, but Netflix position themselves as entertainment providers and could adapt much better
  • Oli Mival: UR is a Higher dimension being: “we reveal things that people can’t see themselves”
  • Approach the problem space with goals, what you want to find out, instead of hypotheses
  • Get an ideas bank/storage: you don’t know yet if it’s useful yet, but you might revisit this when the right technology becomes available (Oli Mival)
  • You need to present your idea in a mental model that people understand
  • Where to invest? What are the things that won’t change? This is where you should invest (your design principle is an example of things worth investing in as they won’t change)

Key Experience indicators

Require well thought out Goals, Signals and Metrics

  1. Goals - What is the outcome you are aiming towards?
    What are the product/service/feature goals that you want to ensure you are achieving?
    Use the KEI framework for inspiration if useful
  2. Signals - What indicates progress towards the goal?
    What shows that you are moving closer to (leading signals) or further from (lagging signals) your goal?
    Could be something the user is doing, thinking, or feeling
  3. Metrics - How do you effectively measure the signals?
    Should be something that is measurable that you can track over time, and you can take action as a result of

Golden rules:

  • KEI’s will never explain themselves. To understand a KEI, one must invest in qualitative research
  • Only measure things that you have the intent and ability to change
  • Measuring everything is measuring nothing. Focus on measuring that are truly central to the experience
  • Get agreement within the team that a certain KEI is important. Disagreement results in ignoring KEIs and not taking action. Create a shared understanding and acceptance of what’s being measured
  • Be careful not to measure behaviour through attitude. Don’t ask about behaviour in a survey. Instead, measure it directly
