Books I've read - my notes

The Smarter Screen - Surprising ways to influence and improve online behaviour - Shlomo Benartzi

Mental screen

  • focus on the mental, not the physical screen
  • factor in the attention environment
  • use information compression techniques
  • incorporate attention filters

A wealth of information creates a poverty of attention.

Being poor means coping not just with a shortfall of money, but also with a concurrent shortfall of cognitive resources. The scientists estimate that the congitive impact of poverty is roughly equvalent to losing a full night of sleep or suffering a 13 point drop in IQ scores. (page 24)

At a certain point, the amount of information doesn't just create a scaricity of attention - it actually makes it harder to deploy whatever attention we have left. If everythin gis impotant then nothing is. (page 27)

We need to treat attention as a literal resource

Function follows form

  • "Fast-test" your site
  • maximise visceral beauty
  • when in doubt, err on the side of simplicity
  • remember: function follows form

Display biases

  • optimise the use of hot spots
  • avoid cold spots
  • consider cultural differences in hotspot location
  • factor in the horinzontal bias
  • sometimes zoom out, not in

New mirror

  • calibrate the amount of feedback
  • use just-in-time education
  • leverage the fresh start effect
  • make us feel the feedback
  • use carrots (and not sticks) whenever possible
  • be aware of social media conformity effect
  • take screen anonymity into account
  • empower feedback with an action plan

Desirable difficulty

  • consider ugly fonts (and other forms of visual disfluency)
  • use congitive disfluency to slow the mind down
  • calibrate disfluency to fit your site's goals

Digital tailoring

  • use personalise visuals and videos
  • tailor the timing of messages
  • tailor the menus to each individual
  • offer more than one Web site
  • remember: too much personalisation can backfire

Choice opportunity

  • offer a manageable consideration set
  • personalise categories
  • incorporate choice tournaments
  • manage regret with choice closure
  • avoid navigation overload
  • manage attribute overload
  • maximise satisfaction, not clicks

Thinking apps

  • start with choice architecture
  • set the right information environment
  • offer thinking tools

General

  • use science to drive your design and A/B testing
  • conduct a whiteboard exercice periodically
  • remember: the medium shapes the message
