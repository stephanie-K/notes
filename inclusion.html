Champions

It's often the same thing for SD and A11Y for example'.

Some examples from Service Week panel discussion: Building Service Design buy in and capability (Link to Thread):

  • Get senior people interested and keen to support by showing how the approach works in a service redesign or project
  • Build a community of people across services who are keen to work in this way and work in the open with them
  • Build the approach into data to day business by ensuring it is included in service reviews, project guidance & improvement work, using #SAtSD maturity model SAtSD webpage and refer to Audit Scotland report.
  • Find a champion who has a louder voice than you and ask them to use it
  • Let your work speak for you, sometimes telling people you used SD approaches AFTER the fact can be more powerful
  • Get promoting, socials, case studies, blogs

Remote working and the opportunity for inclusion

Inclusivity is not a bolt on (34min) you need to plan in every aspect (For example: fire safety, how can a blind person exit safely - what's the plan? even if you don't have a blind employee yet)

Do you have a process for a member of staff transitioning?

Have a conversation with your employees without being nosy: what do you need? what do you have at the moment? is this enough? what might you need? how do you link to communication? what works for you? Get to know your staff better, but leave the private, private.
